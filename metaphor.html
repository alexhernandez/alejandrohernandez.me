Metaphor

A design language built from the ground up to ensure all web apps maintain consistency with CSU Northridge (CSUN) branding and identity standards.

Product Owner · Product Designer · Front End Developer · Mentor

Clients

  • META+LAB
  • Academic Affairs
  • CSU Northridge

Challenge

CSUN has a suite of products all designed and developed by different organizations across the university. A major problem facing all products was a lack of branding consistency, continued re-development of interface elements and the absence of a proper mobile experience. Our vision was to create a design language that could streamline the experience for users and unify product teams across multiple product lines.

Faculty Profiles

Provides CSUN faculty members with a professional and customizable web presence to enhance communication and collaboration among professors, and researchers.

Ask Matty

Ask Matty is a chat bot resource dedicated to answering questions about enrollment, clubs, organizations and other frequently asked campus questions.

Electronic Assessment System

Allows students to submit copies of their assignments for future course, program, and University assessment, which helps the university meet student learning objectives.

Upper Division Writing Proficiency Exam

Commissioned by the Department of Undergraduate Studies, this product allows CSUN students to take the Upper Division Writing Proficiency Exam electronicaly.

Electronic Thesis & Dissertation

Allows graduate students to submit their thesis and dissertations electronically. It creates an end-to-end online workflow for the creation and approval of a master’s thesis, eliminating the need for paper submissions and manual routing.

Late Add Drop

An application to automate and fast-track the late-add drop of classes at CSUN

Employee Self-Identification System (AA2)

Manages electronic self-identification forms used by incoming faculty and lecturers.

Research

Our largest user demographic was college students from the ages of 18-24, which made up 43.7% of our total active users. We conducted a user study with 25 college student participants between the ages of 18-24. This user demographic helped us reinforce that lack of cohesive design and a sub par mobile experience was a major reason for their struggle.

Approach

Because we had a large undertaking we decided to focus only on the 3 major needs of our applications, so we consolidated all product components and prioritized our redesign by frequency and importance.


#1. Type & Palette

We reinforced the universitites branding guidelines by keeping consistent typography & color.


#2. Mobile First

We architected everything from the ground up to transform the mobile user experience.


#3. UI Components

We crafted every component individually to steer clear of inconsistent interface elements.
